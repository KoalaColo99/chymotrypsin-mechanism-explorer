Structure overview

The active site brings remote sequence positions into one functional pocket. This map is schematic, not a scale protein structure.

Chymotrypsin lowers activation barriers but does not change the overall ΔG or reaction equilibrium.

The tetrahedral states represent high-energy, transition-state-like regions—not stable, isolatable products.

Mechanism Accuracy Notes Instructor only
  • His57 activates Ser195 but does not attack the substrate carbonyl.
  • Asp102 organizes and stabilizes His57 rather than directly attacking substrate.
  • The first product leaves during acylation.
  • The acyl–enzyme contains a real covalent Ser195–O–C bond.
  • Water is activated by His57 during deacylation.
  • The oxyanion hole stabilizes both tetrahedral intermediates.
  • The enzyme is regenerated at the end of the cycle.
